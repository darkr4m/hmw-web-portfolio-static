About Me

I was born in Orlando, Florida but raised mostly in the suburbs of Atlanta, GA. Right after I graduated high school in 2010, I joined the United States Army and served in South Korea with the MOS of 94E, which is also known as a Communications Security Repairer. While in Army training I learned how to solder, build computers, troubleshoot, and fix broken equipment. After graduating my job training program, I quickly found out that my particular job was mostly pushing paperwork and quarantining outdated equipment. After a few months I was placed in the headquarters building of my detachment to work as an HR specialist. Soon enough I found myself working another level higher in the battalion headquarters in the S-1 department, where sensitive personnel documents and paperwork was kept and processed. I was placed in charge of processing soldier awards for the entire battalion. After leaving the military, I started college but didn't yet know what I wanted to do quite yet. I knew that I had always liked computers, starting at an early age. Fun fact: I taught myself basic HTML and CSS at the age of twelve. Remember MySpace? I spent a lot of my free time back in the day making custom profiles for myself and for my friends. I think that is where my passion for coding began.

Learning never stops
